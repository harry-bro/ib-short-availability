> This is take-home task for the position of Quantitative Researcher at

> Goals
>
> This is a composite task designed both to test the requirements for
> the job and allow you to showcase your strengths across a few
> different domains, namely:
>
> Exploring the unknown data problem on a macro level (what kind of data
> is this) and micro level (how is this specific dataset structured)
>
> Coding skills (with or without LLM assistants -- see below in more
> detail) and the ability to structure the data processing for
> downstream analysis.
>
> Financial intuition and judgement, both deriving from your "literature
> review" and from analyzing the data in question.
>
> We do not expect candidates to perform equally well in each area.
> There is a minimum level that you will need to demonstrate, but beyond
> that we expect different candidates to have different strengths. In
> this sense, this is not a standardized task, though we do have a long
> list of things you could do within the boundaries of the task that we
> will be using for grading the results.
>
> Timings
>
> A lot of times, the description says that take-home "should take a few
> hours", which subtly implies that you are a weak candidate if you
> cannot finish it in the suggested timeframe. In fact, we know that
> many of you will spend a lot more time on this than others, either
> because they had zero experience with one of the different kinds of
> challenges this tasks poses, or because you were more interested in
> the theme of the task, or simply because you are naturally predisposed
> to exploration. In fact, while knowing where to stop is a very
> important ability, this is often one of the things that comes with
> experience, not a realistic expectation for somebody seeking an entry
> level position. With that in mind, we can only admit that take-home is
> an imposition on your time, and try to speed things elsewhere in your
> hiring process to compensate.
>
> LLMs
>
> It is inconceivable not to have a position on LLM-generated code (or,
> indeed, research) in this day and age. We expect that you will be
> using these tools, and indeed think that they are quite useful across
> all steps of the task. We will also provide paid subscriptions to LLM
> solutions on request, to equalize the playing field in this regard. Be
> advised, however, that even the best models out there function best as
> assistants, rather than automatons, and while the quality of the "full
> autopilot" solution to the task is not zero, it is also below what we
> expect many of you achieve if you put your mind to the task (with or
> without LLM assistance). In this sense, pure LLM solution is almost
> guaranteed to be uncompetitive.
>
